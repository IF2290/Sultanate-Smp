Historical Timeline

  • 1 CE: Common Era is deemed to have begun.
  • 20 CE: Macedonian Empire splits into Roman Empire and Seleucid Empire.
  • 159 CE: Seleucid Empire becomes a Christian-majority empire.
  • 261 CE: Seleucid becomes part of the Roman Empire.
  • 680 CE: First War of Kaikuabad; Persia defeats Islamic Caliphate.
  • 718 CE: Second War of Kaikuabad; Islamic Caliphate defeats Persia.
  • 722–747 CE: Series of Caliphate invasions across Arnavali, Damascus, Jerusalem, and Egypt.
  • 756 CE: Roman capital shifts to Hablyvania and Constantinople.
  • 836 CE: Caliphate reaches peak after fall of Roman northern frontier.
  • 850 CE: Rise of Four Christian Empires; multiple kingdoms and dynasties formed.
  • 852 CE: Hablyvania becomes center of the Church; Raphael elected Pope.
  • 905–1124 CE: Formation of new kingdoms, dynasties, and colonial empires.
  • 1145 CE: Americas discovered by Christopher Magellan.
  • 1196–1200 CE: Rise of West-Mongolian Empire and major dynastic changes.
  • 1210–1216 CE: Jerusalem annexed, wars between Bybars and Karakura.
  • 1225 CE: Massacre of House of Van Damme; Karljestin annexed.
  • 1233 CE: Zahveth becomes center of Christianity.
  • 1248–1251 CE: Ranex expansion; Crusades begin.
  • 1252–1253 CE: Major wars reshape Latveria, Byzantines, Bybars, and Ranex.
